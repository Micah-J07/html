Key Terms


Current

The rate of flow of electric charge/flow of electrons in a circuit. It is measured in amps (A).

Potential Difference (Voltage)

The difference in the amount of energy that electrons have between two points in a circuit. It is measured in volts (V).

Power

The rate at which something converts energy from one type to another. Power is measured in watts.

Insulator

A material that does not conduct or allow electrical current or thermal (heat) energy to pass through very well. For example, paper, plastic and rubber are all insulators.

Conductor

A material that is capable of easily transferring thermal (heat) energy or electrical charges. For example, silver, copper and steel are all excellent conductors of heat and electricity and are all types of metals. An example of a non-metal conductor is graphite, a form of carbon.

Ammeter

A device that measures current in amps. The symbol is an "A" in a circle with two lines coming from it horizontally.

Voltmeter

A device that measures volts/potential difference between two points in a circuit. Voltmeters are always connected in parallel.
